Predicting Concrete Strength

Matt Williams January 5, 2025

Concept

This project is an extension to my project titled Effects of Concrete Mix Design & Aging on Strength. Here, I sought to develop a machine learning model that could predict the 28 day concrete breaking strength based on the sample's mix design. I tested several machine learning algorithms and found that a random forest model resulted in the most accurate strength predictions.

Data

Concrete Compressive Strength [creative commons] from UC Irvine

Process

  1. Read data into a Pandas dataframe.
  2. Look for & remove records with missing data.
  3. Explore the relationship of the mix ingredients using a seaborn correlation matrix and pair plots.
  4. Remove records with outlier attribute values.
  5. Split the data 80% for training and 20% for testing.
  6. Create a scaled copy of the data for the ML models that require it.
  7. Train & test scikit-learn machine learning models.
  8. Compare model performance.
  9. Tune model performance of top two performing models (Random Forest & MLP) by doing feature selection and hyperparameter tuning.

Results

The correlation matrix (shown here) highlighted several interesting relationships. Most notably is the negative correlation between water and the superplasticizer additive. Second to that, the cement content and concrete strength showed a positive correlation.

The best performing model was the random forest model which produced a model score of 0.89. A plot of the predicted and actual breaking strengths from the random forest model is shown here.
